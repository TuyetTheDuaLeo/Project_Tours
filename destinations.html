In the Northern part of Vietnam, Hanoi and Halong Bay are the two main highlights that are truly worth your time and budget. The two cities are increasingly connecting via the newly developed highways. It now takes only 2 hours to get from Hanoi to Halong Bay via the Hanoi - Hai Phong expressway (toll fees applied).

There are daily flights connecting Singapore and Danang, operated by Vietnam Airlines, Singapore Airlines and Jet Star. The flight takes 2.5 hours each way.

Hoi An is best seen in February - May when the weather is not yet too hot and there is fewer rain. Activities to enjoy in Hoi An includes cycling tours, day trips to local villages, beach relaxation or a simple walk around the old town. Hoi An is also famous for its lantern making tradition, as well as same-day-collecting tailor shop.

For 4 weeks, you can experience the major parts of Vietnam for sure. Consider the below itinerary for example:

Northern part of Vietnam: Hanoi (3 days), Ninh Binh (1 day), Mai Chau (2 days), Sapa (3 days), Halong Bay (3 days) - total: 12 days

Central part of Vietnam: Phong Nha (2 days), Hue (2 days), Danang (3 days), Hoi An (3 days), Nha Trang (2 days) - total: 12 days

Southern part of Vietnam: Ho Chi Minh City (2 days), Mekong Delta (2 days)
